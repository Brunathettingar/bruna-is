Seven service areas.

From fire sealing and fireproofing to pipe insulation, aluminium cladding and pipe markings — Brunaþéttingar offer complete technical insulation for buildings across Iceland.

Services in full

One company — seven specialties.

Brunaþéttingar provide complete technical insulation for buildings across Iceland. We started in fire sealing in 2011 and have added services as our clients have required — always with the same focus on quality, certification and documentation.

These are the seven areas we serve today. Some services overlap (fire protection and acoustic insulation use the same materials) and some require specialised systems from Isogenopak, WrapTec or Armaflex — but they all fall under technical insulation.

01 / Fire protection

Fireproofing & passive fire sealing

Complete fire protection for buildings — all types of passive fire sealing, intumescent paint for structural steel, fire-rated boards, and seals on penetrations and joints. Certified to EI 60, EI 120 and EI 240 (and R 30 to R 240 for structural protection).

Bonus benefit Fire-rated caulk is highly sound-absorbing — the same seals that stop fire and smoke also dampen sound transmission between rooms. One solution, two functions.
  • Sealing of penetrations, joints and gaps
  • Fire-rated caulks, foams and pillows (Roxtec, Hilti, Promat)
  • Intumescent paint for structural steel
  • Fire boards, fire-rated insulation, fire-rated doors
  • Smoke sealing and air-pressure testing
02 / Pipe insulation

Pipe insulation — Isogenopak, WrapTec & Armaflex

Insulation on hot and cold pipes — on one hand to prevent heat loss and on the other to prevent moisture dripping from cold pipes. We work with three leading systems: Isogenopak PVC cladding for indoor pipes, WrapTec self-welding jacketing for outdoor pipes, and Armaflex closed-cell foam for cooling pipes.

Ideal in kitchens and schools Isogenopak is clean, smooth and beautifully finished cladding — ideal in kitchens, daycares, hospitals or anywhere the appearance of pipe insulation matters. No dust falls from exposed mineral wool.
  • Heat and cooling insulation on hot and cold pipes
  • Anti-condensation insulation on cold pipes (prevents drips)
  • Isogenopak PVC cladding — smooth, dust-free, for indoor use
  • WrapTec self-welding jacketing for outdoor pipes — 100% watertight
  • Armaflex closed-cell foam for cooling pipes and refrigeration
  • Multiplies the lifespan of the insulation — energy savings pay back the investment
03 / Ventilation

Ventilation insulation — for fire or sound

Insulation on ventilation ducts in buildings for two purposes: fire-rated insulation (EI 30, EI 60 or EI 120) or sound dampening between floors and rooms. The same systems also provide thermal insulation and energy savings as a bonus.

Two functions in one Fire-rated insulation on ducts also dampens sound transmission between rooms — one installation, two functions. That's why it's economical to do both at the same time.
  • Fire-rated insulation on ducts — EI 30 / EI 60 / EI 120
  • Acoustic insulation on ducts between floors and rooms
  • Thermal insulation — reduces heat loss on supply ducts
  • Pressure sealing on duct joints
  • Indoor and outdoor solutions
04 / Water tanks

Water tank insulation

Insulation on water tanks and hot storage tanks so they don't lose temperature. This is one of our most cost-effective solutions — the investment often pays back in months through lower energy bills.

Pays back in 6–18 months An uninsulated water tank loses 15–25% of its heat every 24 hours. Simple insulation stops nearly all of that loss — the investment returns very quickly.
  • Hot water tanks — prevents heat loss
  • Cold water tanks — protects against condensation and dust
  • Industrial tanks and basement storage
  • Finished with Isogenopak or aluminium cladding
  • Annual energy-saving calculation provided per project
05 / Markings

Pipe markings

Colour-coded pipe markings for all piping systems in a building — hot water, cold water, steam lines, gas, drainage, sprinklers, fuel and more. Many plumbers skip this step and bring in fire sealers or technical insulators to complete the marking work.

Requirement, not optional Pipe markings are mandatory in industrial buildings and critical structures under Icelandic standards — and a practical safety requirement for fire authorities, maintenance crews and facility operators.
  • Colour coding per ÍST / EN 1130-1 and BS 1710
  • Medium type, flow direction and temperature
  • Markings on insulated or bare pipes
  • Indoor and outdoor, in industry and commercial buildings
  • Delivered together with a report and pipe map for the owner
06 / Aluminium cladding

Aluminium jacket & cladding

Strong, durable aluminium cladding that wraps insulation on pipes, ducts and industrial equipment — indoors and outdoors. Protects the underlying insulation from weather and damage and preserves the energy efficiency of the building for decades of operation.

Industrial standard in Iceland The image to the right shows aluminium-clad steam pipes from the Hellisheiði geothermal plant — the same systems we install in industry, power plants and outdoor insulation across Iceland.
  • Aluminium cladding on insulated pipes and ducts
  • Outdoor insulation in power plants, industry and factories
  • Pre-formed bends and T-pieces (ordered from a partner, installed by us)
  • Watertight joints and weather-resistant finish
  • Preserves the energy efficiency of the insulation for decades
07 / Advisory

Advisory & material selection

Maybe you don't need us to install anything — maybe you just need advice. We offer independent advisory services for contractors, owners, fire engineers and operators: material selection, quantity take-off from drawings, solution design for complex projects, and independent inspections of existing fire sealing.

We don't design — Tensio does Fire engineering design (by certified engineers) is not our discipline and we don't intend to take it on. We work with fire engineers and specifically recommend Tensio Architects — then we handle material selection, planning, installation and reporting.
  • Material selection — when to choose Roxtec vs. Hilti vs. Promat etc.
  • Quantity take-off from drawings and cost estimation
  • Solution design for complex projects that don't fit standard kits
  • Coordination with fire engineers (Tensio and others)
  • Independent inspection reports on existing buildings
Cost effectiveness

Technical insulation that pays itself back.

Technical insulation is one of the most cost-effective investments a building owner can make. The Isogenopak and WrapTec systems we install are particularly economical — they multiply the lifespan of the underlying insulation, reduce maintenance costs and improve energy efficiency over the entire operating life of the building.

We provide an annual energy-saving calculation for every project before you accept the quote — so you know exactly when the investment will pay itself back.
