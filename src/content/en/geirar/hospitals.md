The national hospital and the smaller regional facilities run twenty-four hours, seven days. Fire-sealing and technical-insulation work has to fit between operations, behind closed wards, sometimes through ceiling voids at night — and the documentation has to be airtight, because the fire authority and the hospital's own quality system both audit it.

We deliver per-opening records for every project: photo before, system used, photo after, certificate reference, all pinned to a drawing. Acoustic insulation on ducts between wards, fire sealing on every penetration crossing a compartment line, and intumescent protection on exposed structural steel.

No operations stop because of a fire inspection — that's the bar.
