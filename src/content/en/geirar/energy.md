Iceland's energy infrastructure is steam, hot water and electricity at scale. The Hellisheiði and Krafla geothermal plants, the hydropower stations on the Þjórsá and Kárahnjúkar systems, the district heating tunnels under Reykjavík — every kilometre of pipework needs technical insulation that holds up to temperature and weather.

Our typical scope is aluminium-clad insulation on steam and condensate lines, intumescent protection on supporting steel, and fire sealing at every penetration in substations and switchgear rooms. Outdoor work is finished with WrapTec self-welding jacketing or aluminium cladding, depending on exposure.

We design for a forty-year operating life and document accordingly.
