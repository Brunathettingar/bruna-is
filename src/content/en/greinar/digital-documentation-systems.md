For most of fire-sealing's history, the documentation was a folder of paper certificates handed over at the end of a job. That worked when nothing changed after handover. It does not work when buildings are renovated, when penetrations are added, when ownership changes, and when the fire authority asks for a record five years later.

## Why paper isn't enough

A paper folder is a snapshot at handover. The minute the first new cable is pulled through, the folder is out of date and nobody updates it. By year five it is wrong in a hundred small ways and there is no reliable way to tell which entries still match reality.

## What a digital system should include

A useful digital documentation system for fire sealing covers:

- a per-opening record with photos, system used, installer, date and the certificate it traces to
- the building drawings with each opening pinned to its location
- a change log when an opening is modified, with a new photo and a new certificate reference
- search and export so the fire authority can be answered in minutes, not weeks
