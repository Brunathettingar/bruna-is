The handover report is what separates "the job is done" from "the job is delivered and traceable for thirty years". A good report does two things at once: it proves to the fire authority that the work is compliant today, and it gives the building owner a maintenance baseline they can refer back to as the building changes.

## The twelve items every report should include

1. **Project identification.** Building address, owner, project number, dates, signatures.
2. **As-built drawings.** Every fire-rated wall and slab marked, with each penetration labelled.
3. **Per-opening register.** One record per penetration, joint or seal — location, system, products, fire rating, photos before and after.
4. **Product datasheets.** The ETA or classification document for every product used.
5. **Installer competence records.** Who installed each system and what training they hold.
6. **Inspection record.** Who inspected the work, when, and what was checked.
7. **Test reports.** Where smoke-tightness or air-pressure tests were performed, with results.
8. **Change-log placeholder.** A blank section the owner can use to record future modifications.
9. **Photo set.** A complete photographic record cross-referenced to the register.
10. **Maintenance protocol.** How the owner should inspect, at what frequency, and what triggers a re-seal.
11. **Contact list.** Who to call for warranty work and future modifications.
12. **Hand-signed declaration.** A statement of conformity signed by the responsible installer.

## How our documentation system keeps them together

Each of the twelve items is generated from a single per-opening record in our system. When an opening is added or modified, every section that depends on it updates automatically — no folder of paper to fall out of sync.
