Roxtec, Hilti, Promat and Beele are the four manufacturers we work with most often. Each has its strengths, each has gaps, and none of them is the right choice for every situation. Here is how we think about each.

## Roxtec

Roxtec is the reference standard for modular cable and pipe transit. The certification depth is unmatched and the ETAs cover combinations no one else does. **Strengths:** documentation, flexibility, retrofitting. **Trade-offs:** higher cost per opening.

## Hilti

Hilti's strength is the integrated catalogue — caulks, sealants, foams, fire-rated mortars, board systems, and a published BIM library that makes design coordination simple. **Strengths:** ecosystem, BIM support, contractor availability. **Trade-offs:** not always the deepest tested combinations for unusual configurations.

## Promat

Promat is strongest in board and intumescent paint systems — structural steel protection, fire-rated cladding, ductwork enclosures. **Strengths:** structural fire protection, depth of board catalogue. **Trade-offs:** narrower penetration-seal range than the others.

## Beele

Beele is a specialist for the most demanding environments — petrochemical, marine, infrastructure — where additional requirements (gas-tight, water-tight, blast) sit alongside fire. **Strengths:** extreme-condition certification. **Trade-offs:** cost and lead time for ordinary commercial scope.

## When we use each

For most commercial buildings we mix products from these manufacturers based on what each opening needs. The full article will walk through specific situations and the choice we make for each.
