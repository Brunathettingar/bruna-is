Cable trays are one of the hardest penetrations to seal well. They carry varying cable loads over the life of the building, they bundle dozens of cables of different diameters, and the seal has to keep its fire rating while still allowing future changes. The right product depends on how often the bundle will change.

## Modular cable transit

Modular transit systems (Roxtec, Beele and similar) use a frame around the cables and adaptable rubber modules sized to each cable diameter. They are the strongest choice when:

- the cable run will change over the building's life
- there are tight requirements for water or gas tightness alongside fire
- you need an audit-friendly system with clear per-opening documentation

## Caulks and foams

Fire-rated caulks, sealants and foams are simpler and cheaper, and they handle small or one-off penetrations well. They work best when:

- the cable bundle is fixed and will not change
- the opening is small and irregular
- there is no requirement beyond the fire rating itself

## Pillows

Cushion-style fire pillows are the right choice when access is needed often — for example in temporary IT rooms or staged build-outs. They install quickly without tools, are easy to remove and reinstall, and accept new cables without rework. The trade-off is that they need more frequent inspection over time.

## Choosing

For most commercial buildings we recommend modular transit at the main spine penetrations and a mix of caulk and pillows on secondary openings. The decision should be made together with the electrical contractor before MEP routes are fixed.
