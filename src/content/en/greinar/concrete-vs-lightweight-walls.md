The same fire-sealing product does not behave the same way in a concrete wall as it does in a lightweight (gypsum on steel-stud) wall. The wall's own thermal mass and rigidity are part of the tested system, so the same penetration in a different wall is a different system entirely.

## Concrete walls

Concrete and CMU walls have high thermal mass. They draw heat out of the seal during a fire and slow the temperature rise on the unexposed face. They also do not move with temperature. Most fire-rated mortars, foams and caulks are tested in concrete and perform best there.

## Lightweight walls

Lightweight walls have low thermal mass. The seal itself has to do more of the work, and it has to handle the stud movement that comes when the wall heats up. Caulks and intumescent sealants are typically required at deeper sections than the concrete equivalent, and the backing material matters.

## What this means in practice

A product certificate that reads "tested in 150 mm concrete to EI 90" tells you nothing about the same product in a 100 mm gypsum partition. Always check the ETA wall type before specifying.
